About Open APP Explorer

Open APP Explorer is an independent tool designed to help UK higher education professionals explore and compare Access and Participation Plan (APP) targets across universities.

What is an APP?

Access and Participation Plans are agreements between universities and the Office for Students (OfS) that set out how institutions will improve equality of opportunity for underrepresented groups - covering access, student success, and progression into employment or further study.

Where does the data come from?

Targets are extracted from publicly available APP documents published on the OfS. Extraction is carried out using AI, which reads the target sections of each PDF and structures the data into comparable fields. This means occasional errors or gaps are possible - if you spot something incorrect for your institution, please use the feedback form.

Demographic data (IMD, ethnicity, disability, and so on) is drawn from the OfS's own published student characteristic data, matched to each institution by UKPRN. Mission group data was taken from publicly available sources, but is also prone to error.

How current is it?

The APP targets are taken from all 'currently active' APPs. Coverage is not complete - around 20-30% of institutions either had not yet published their APP at the time of extraction, or published it in a format that could not be read automatically.

Who built this?

This tool has been built by David Gilani as an independent project to support the UK higher education access and participation community, with feedback from that community constantly helping to drive it forward. This site is not affiliated with the Office for Students or any other regulatory body.

The hope is that it sparks ideas for better ways to organise public data about universities into the future to aid collaboration between providers and drive the sector towards addressing equity gaps faster than if we go alone.

If you have questions, suggestions, or spot any errors, please use the feedback form.
